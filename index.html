Foundations of Biology I Laboratory (BIO140L), Spring 2025


Course Expectations

Course Description: Students learn the process of science and the basics of experimental design while building scientific writing skills. Students learn foundational techniques of molecular biology (pipetting, DNA extraction, PCR, gel electrophoresis and basic bioinformatics) in the context of a DNA Barcoding project. Students will leave this course with the preparation needed to design their own independent DNA barcoding projects. May be used for general education credit. Prerequisite(s): BIO 140. Corequisite(s): BIO 140.


Upon successful completion of BIO140L we will be able to:

  • Understand and apply the process of science
  • Interpret phylogenetic trees
  • Use basic molecular biology techniques like pipetting, DNA extraction, PCR, gels and DNA sequencing
  • Practice scientific communication and collaboration
  • Design and conduct an independent DNA barcoding project in BIO 150 Lab
  • Demonstrate basic skills commonly expected in molecular biology research and internship opportunities
  • See ourselves as scientists

Grading Scale: Semester grades will be assigned as follows: A ≥ 93.00%, A- ≥ 90.00%, B+ ≥ 87.00%, B ≥ 83.00%, B- ≥ 80.00%, C+ ≥ 77.00%, %, C ≥ 73.00%, C- ≥ 70.00%, D+ ≥ 67%, D ≥ 63.00%, D- ≥ 60.00%, F < 60.00. Final grades will not be rounded!


For many activities/assignments you will work in a group with the other students at your lab bench. For group assignments, each member of the group must contribute equally for each to receive full points. Prelab quizzes are typically due 2 hours before the start of each lab. The lowest Prelab quiz grade will be dropped for each student; please note, this does not include the Phylogeny quiz, or Checkpoint quizzes.

If a student wishes to appeal an assignment or an exam grade, they must present their case to me within a week of receiving the graded material. After a week, no appeals will be accepted regardless of their merit.

Disruptive Events

Laboratory Attendance: On-time attendance in lab is required. All the labs will be carried out in small groups, so missing a lab will put you AND your group members at a serious disadvantage.


Respiratory and Intestinal Viruses including Norovirus and Covid: DO NOT come to lab if you are vomiting/have diarrhea and/or have a fever. You should be fever-free and/or intestinal symptom-free for 24 hours before returning to class. Please consider wearing a mask if you have an active cough. Contact your instructor right away and explain the circumstances, and they will work with you to make up any missed work.


What do I do If I miss a Lab? If you must miss a lab due to a planned, verifiable reason, it must be cleared by your lab instructor in advance and you must make arrangements to work with your lab instructor on completing the missed material. Students are expected to make up any missed assignments or assessments. Please note that as specified in JMU Academic Affairs Policy #16, accommodations will be made for court-imposed legal obligations, religious observances, active military service, and official University functions. In all cases documentation must be provided to the instructor within one week of the missed lab. If you are ill, please contact the instructor as soon as possible to devise a plan for making up the missed lab. It is not necessary that the instructor be informed of the nature of the illness or emergency.


Please email your instructor if you are experiencing extenuating circumstances. If you miss too much class time due to unexpected health-related or other circumstances, an incomplete or class withdrawal option can be considered depending on the situation. For extended absences, students are urged to contact the Office of the Dean of Students.


Absences not approved by your instructor are considered unexcused. The first unexcused lab absence will result in a zero for the quiz and any other items due that day. A second unexcused absence will result in a 10% deduction from the semester lab course grade. A third unexcused absence will result in a zero for the semester lab course grade. Tardiness to lab is also not acceptable. If a student is 20 minutes late to lab on three days, that will count as one unexcused absence.


Inclement Weather: If the university opens late due to a weather delay, labs may start at that opening time. If labs are canceled due to bad weather, they will be made up as early as possible in the same week, which may be on the Saturday immediately following the missed day. Announcements concerning canceled labs will be posted on Canvas usually within 12 hours of the cancellation. It is your responsibility to make certain that you are receiving Canvas notifications.
